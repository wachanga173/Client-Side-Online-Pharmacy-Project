1. Introduction

Welcome to Pharmacare ("we," "our," or "us"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and the security of your personal health information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our online pharmacy services.

As a healthcare provider and online pharmacy, we comply with:

  • The Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019
  • Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) standards
  • Pharmacy Board of Kenya regulations
  • International data protection best practices

By using our services, you consent to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our services.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information

When you register for an account or use our services, we may collect:

  • Identity Information: Full name, date of birth, gender, government ID number
  • Contact Information: Email address, phone number, mailing address, delivery address
  • Account Information: Username, password (encrypted), security questions
  • Payment Information: Credit/debit card details, billing address, payment method preferences

2.2 Protected Health Information (PHI)

As a pharmacy, we collect sensitive health information including:

  • Prescription Information: Medication names, dosages, prescriber details, prescription numbers
  • Medical History: Allergies, current medications, past prescriptions, health conditions
  • Insurance Information: Insurance provider, policy numbers, coverage details
  • Healthcare Provider Information: Doctor names, contact information, medical facility details
  • Clinical Notes: Pharmacist consultations, medication counseling records

2.3 Technical Information

We automatically collect certain information when you use our website:

  • IP address and device information
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent on pages
  • Clickstream data and navigation patterns
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies

2.4 Communications

  • Emails, chat messages, and phone call records with customer service
  • Feedback, reviews, and survey responses
  • Messages to and from healthcare providers

4. HIPAA Compliance

HIPAA Protection: As a covered healthcare entity, we follow HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) standards to protect your Protected Health Information (PHI).

4.1 Your Rights Under HIPAA

You have the right to:

  • Access: Request copies of your health records
  • Amendment: Request corrections to your health information
  • Accounting: Receive an accounting of disclosures of your PHI
  • Restrictions: Request restrictions on how we use your PHI
  • Confidential Communications: Request we contact you in specific ways
  • Notice: Receive a copy of our Notice of Privacy Practices

4.2 Permitted Uses and Disclosures

We may use and disclose your PHI without authorization for:

  • Treatment: Coordinating your care with healthcare providers
  • Payment: Processing insurance claims and billing
  • Healthcare Operations: Quality improvement and pharmacy operations
  • Public Health: Reporting as required by law
  • Safety: Preventing serious threats to health or safety

4.3 Notice of Privacy Practices

Our full HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices is available upon request. Contact us at privacy@pharmacare.com to receive a copy.

If Pharmacare is involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred. We will notify you before your information becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

6. Data Security

We implement comprehensive security measures to protect your information:

6.1 Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption: 256-bit SSL/TLS encryption for data transmission
  • Data Storage: Encrypted databases with restricted access
  • Passwords: Hashed and salted password storage
  • Firewalls: Network firewalls and intrusion detection systems
  • Monitoring: 24/7 security monitoring and threat detection

6.2 Administrative Safeguards

  • Role-based access controls (staff access only what's needed)
  • Background checks for all pharmacy personnel
  • Regular security training for employees
  • HIPAA compliance officer oversight
  • Incident response procedures

6.3 Physical Safeguards

  • Secure data centers with restricted physical access
  • Locked prescription storage areas
  • Secure disposal of physical records
  • Video surveillance of pharmacy facilities

6.4 Data Breach Notification

In the unlikely event of a data breach affecting your information, we will:

  • Notify you within 72 hours of discovering the breach
  • Describe the nature of the breach and data affected
  • Explain steps we're taking to address the breach
  • Provide guidance on protecting yourself
  • Report to relevant authorities as required by law
